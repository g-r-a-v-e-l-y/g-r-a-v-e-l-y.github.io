Eastern Quake League:


Well some bad news rolled around to us last night. It seems we have been removed from the EQL playoffs. The replacement for us is unknown at this point in time. All in all i cant say this was unexpected, could we have realistically competed and won? Probably not. To my understanding though, (correct me if im wrong) but we were a replacement for the now defunct clan 5am. We assumed their points and their standings. Now my question I have is if 5am had not disbanded would they have been removed from the playoffs? If we had assumed "their" record and "their" points, then i honestly dont see what the problem is.

I dont think the EQL intentionally did this to make people mad but they had to look at it in the fairest way possible. That was to let people into the playoffs that had been in EQL from the beginning. But unfortunately they did not plan on clans disbanding and dropping EQL. So they were forced to make a judgement call. To make it fair though let the teams that are in contention play each other for that spot. That way there is no question who deserves to be there.

osp + q3comp:


The two big boys of regulation quake 3 play, q3comp and osp, are merging!

OSP has putt out version 0.99s and is running rapidly out of letters to use before a 1.0 release. Major changes include the first starts at adding in q3comp featurs to osp. Most of you already have some sort of 99s pk3 autodownloaded from a badlands server, it is the standard badlands has gone gold on and will be using on every officel match.

Check here for a list of changes and if you don't already have it, get it now!

Most interesting to me is the following:

- Add: Windowed stats option. To view current stats while playing
or following a player, bind a key to +wstats. A sliding window
will pop out showing your current game stats. This will also
show at intermission below the scoreboard for complete match
summary on a single screen. In multiview, the stats of the player
of the highlighted window will be displayed.

Leet - for those of you that don't toy with new options like I do, you may have missed osp's "/menu" command. Look into it. it's great.
